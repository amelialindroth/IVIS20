Discovery process 1

Are countries who value family and friends happier?

I choose wave 4 (1995-1999) to find this out.

Immediately some countries stand out.

It turns out to be Ukraine, Belarus and Russia. They seem to be less happy than all the other countries. However, it is really hard to get an overview of the countries in relation to each other.

Now let's look at the ''Is family important?'' variable. Here it is even harder to make anything out. Let's switch tools! Below Russia, Ukraine and Belarus stand out again. Albania also seems like an unhappy country.

It does not seem that the countries in question value friends and family much less than the rest of the countries.

However, when brushing and only showing Europe and then Americas there seems to be a positive correlation between valuing friends and being happy.

This positive correlation is also present between happiness and state of health, and happiness and life expectancy so we should not draw any conclusions about causation.

To conclude, yes, it seems that there is a positive correlation between happiness and thinking friends are important but we can not say definitively that this is a causal relationship.

Discovery process 2

What affects how important politics is for a country?

Starting out by looking at wave 4 (1995-1999) and the Clean election index (which perhaps seems obvious), having corrupt elections does not seem to affect whether or not politics are important. It does however seem that countries with a higher index also think politics are more important.

Continuing to look at how much confidence they have in the government, the confidence does not seem to be an indicating factor. Japan, for instance, values politics but does not have confidence in their government. On the other end Azerbaijan has a lot of confidence in their government but does not value politics as much (there may be other reasons for their confidence to be high but that is another subject).

Interestingly enough, only the happier countries think politics are important. There is also a positive correlation present among the Asian countries.

Thinking that friends and leisure time are important seem to be positively correlated too. Perhaps this is a cultural phenomenon? That if a country vales one category high they do the others?

No conclusions can be drawn from my investigation into this subject, but I do think it is an interesting one.
